What is a Reflection?

Refers to the process of contemplating or thinking deeply about one's own thoughts, experiences, actions, and emotions. It involves introspection and examination of oneself in relation to past events, situations, or interactions. Reflection often leads to insights, understanding, and self-awareness, enabling individuals to learn from their experiences, make informed decisions, and grow personally and professionally.

Power Learning

Reflection Paper #2

Studying the POWER Learning strategy in my study skills class has given me excellent insights into efficient learning methods. POWER is an acronym for Prepare, Organize, Work, Evaluate, and Rethink. Each stage in this strategy is important, since it guided my learning through  organized approach to learning. This topic has taught me the need of comprehensive preparation before beginning any learning work, ensuring that I have all of the essential materials and a clear knowledge of what needs to be done. Organizing my study materials and establishing a favorable learning environment has proved to help me focus and be more productive. The "Work" phase in my study skills class focused on active involvement with the content, using tactics such as summarizing, questioning, and elaborating to increase comprehension. Following that, the "Evaluate" stage pushed me to review my success and find areas for improvement, allowing me to change my strategy as necessary. Finally, the "Rethink" step encouraged my reflection on my learning process, helping me improve my techniques and adapt to new obstacles. let's consider a scenario where I have an upcoming exam in a challenging subject like Finance. First, I would prepare by reviewing my notes and textbooks, to ensure I have a comprehensive understanding of the concepts. Next, I would organize my study materials, creating a dedicated study space free from distractions for example let's say i chose the kitchen. During the "Work" phase, I would actively engage with the material by practicing problems, for examples I would practice solving for analysis ratios for say, and teaching the concepts to myself. As I progress, I would evaluate my understanding by testing myself with practice quizzes and reviewing my performance. Finally, in the "Rethink" phase, I would reflect on my study sessions, identifying any areas where I struggled. By following the POWER Learning method in this scenario, I can approach my exam with confidence, knowing that I've employed effective study strategies to maximize my learning outcomes.

I chose the topic because because I want to be positive and I feel this self-esteem teaching helps me to generate belief in myself and this have been helping me achieve my goals.

Conclusion
